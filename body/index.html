How does Multiple Sclerosis Affect the Body?

Multiple Sclerosis is a neurodegenerative and inflammatory immune condition which causes problems throughout the body. Researchers happen to believe that Multiple Sclerosis causes the body's immune system to attack myelin, the insulating coating around nerve cells. As the myelin erodes, communication between nerve cells in the central nervous system is broken or disrupted. When this happens, parts of the body do not receive instructions from the central nervous system, the controlling system of the body. Types of Multiple Sclerosis

  • Relapsing-remitting: Multiple Sclerosis comes and goes over time. Symptoms may be severe but then happen to disappear.
  • Secondary-progressive: After the first attack, the disease may start to progress into more deliberate ways.
  • Primary-progressive: People who develop Multiple Sclerosis later in their life. They happen to decline slowly, without having to face many ups and downs.
  • Progressive relapsing: Symptoms initially progress slowly but eventually worsen over time.
